Cyberfeminist Manifesto Generator (2015)

Currently featured at Belvedere 21 Museum of Contemporary Art.

An online manifesto generator that determines how one’s ideology aligns with the various cyberfeminist movements based upon a series of questions. The questions are pre-determined, implying that many people actually believe in the same things, or slight variations thereupon. The piece aims to expose and satirize one of the paradoxes of the ‘buzzfeed generation’ of online quizzes, where the implication is that everything – even complex belief systems – can be determined by a handful of simple questions and their responses.

SP0RE (2016)

Digital Installation, Audio, Projection, Video Capture

This installation allows the audience to create a digital self portrait by taking a picture of themselves. This image is then added to a new digital environment that allows the audience member to transcend their human form and occupy a different environment as a digital life-form. Self-portraiture has existed since the beginning of human existence and remains an important part of self expression today. This piece examines how individuals choose to represent themselves and explores the selfie out of the context of social media.
